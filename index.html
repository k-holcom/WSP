Header

Target Audience

The target audience are homeowners. The average age range of homeowners who would be targeted is between 34 and 70. The users of the site would be within a 50 mile radius of the handy man. This would also be based on his specific skills (which were not listed on the template).

Client/User Needs

The client would like to have a site that is able to relay his skills and availability. He would want his clients to be able to contact him about his services, read about his business, and about his services.

Users need a simple way to find if he is the right man for the job. Therefore, they need something that is straight-forward and able to find his services and ways to contact him.

Design Critique

In the header, there are four main colors. First, there is the color black. I do not think that the designer for this site intended on there to be a meaning behind the color. The Orange in the header (under the active page link) is used to make the active page stand out and make sure the user knows which page they are on. They yellow that is in the logo shows optimism and to draw the user to the name.

Fullscreen Map

Target Audience

The target Audience of a site that would use this component would be those between the ages of 18 and around 40 or 50. The locale of users would vary. Most users would be local within 50 miles, but the possibilities are really nationwide due to the ease of entering an address.

Client/User Needs

Client needs are simple. They need to allow their users to see where they are located. This is shown by the pointer, and when the pointer is clicked(not shown) a pop-up gives the company's information such as name, address, phone, and any other pertinent information.

Design Critique

This design places focus on the map itself. By taking up the entire width of the screen, it is saying to the user that the company's location is very important. Also by having the darker map, it has a sort of elegance or sophistication to it.

Social Media Links

Target Audience

Men and women between the ages of 18 and 49. There is no location specific to this component as with social media it can reach world-wide.

Client/User Needs

The client who wants this feature is wanting their "name" to be spread widely. Social Media has a tendency to be as good if not better than word of mouth. When one person follows, they post about it and other see it and it increases the Client's business.

Design Critique

The design of this is very good. First the color brings attention to the area. As a client you want users to be directed toward specific areas and with social media, word spreads like wild fire. Therefore a client would want this to grab the user's attention. This example does so with the bright color. The color green is the color that represents communication and being social. This is a perfect choice for linking to the company's social media accounts.

Mobile CTA

Target Audience

The majority of users would be mothers with children between the age of 4 to 18 within a 50 mile radius. Unless the user is willing to travel farther for the gym.

Client/User Needs

The Client wants to attract new customers by letting them know about the pros of starting gymnastics, and the differences in the different types.

Design Critique

The Mobile CTA is simple yet straight to the point. The blue background portrays that the gym is kind, trying to portray the truth and the devotion needed for gymnastics. The font used is a sans-serif font and this can be seen as the gym is wanting to portray a smooth, simple, and sleek appearance. Finally, the images of both acrobatic gymnastics and the lesser known rhythmic gymnastics. This ties in to the tag-line "Get to know everything about gymnastics".

Footer

Target Audience

This company is attempting to reach those between the ages of approx 25 to 45. These are the ages when people are more apt to create a company and need the services of a web designer.

Client/User Needs

Users need simplicity and for the developer to be straight forward with what is available and how they can be helped.

Design Critique

The footer is what most people think of when they think of a footer. They have the basic contact information (their email address). However, there where a few things that caught my eye. First of all, the way the developer put the email address. This is a way to grab people's attention. People are use to seeing the @ sign. However, they use [at] instead. Secondly, they pose a question to the user. They ask if they can help me.

News Feed

Target Audience

The target audience here is dog owners between the ages of 24 and 75. The locale of the user is not important beyond the United States.

Client/User Needs

The client is wanting to keep the users up to date on the news/blogs that are written.

They use a very simplistic approach to this. They have a icon that represents either the author or the topic of discussion. They then have the title and brief excerpt of the article. On the side, there is the date that the article was created so they know which article is new and with all of this information, they are able to choose the best article for them and read it.

About Section

Target Audience

Patients and future patients within a 25 mile radius between the ages of 18 and 80.

Client/User Needs

Ability to explain who they are what what they are set out to do.
The users want to be able to see information about the people who they will be trusting with their health and the health of their family's health.

Design Critique

This page is very elegant and well thought out. It is split into 3 sections. A version of the rule of thirds. In the first section they answer the question/statement "Who We Are". There is then a section that is dedicated to answering that. The next section has a brief history of the company. Finally they answer some questions that would arise when someone would ask "Why should we use your company?". This can be found under the "Why Us?" section.
